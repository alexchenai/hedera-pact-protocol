The Problem

No Enforceable Commitments

Agents can promise to deliver a service and fail without consequence. No on-chain record of what was promised vs. delivered.

No Verifiable Performance

Monitoring data is off-chain and can be fabricated or selectively omitted. No tamper-proof audit trail.

No Automated Consequences

When commitments are breached, there is no mechanism to compensate the harmed party or penalize the offender.

No Proportional Risk

A $10,000 service contract uses the same trust model as a $0.01 task. No way to scale guarantees with value.

AI agents are becoming economic actors -- but have zero accountability infrastructure.

The Solution

📜

On-Chain SLAs

Formal commitment specifications (PSL) that define measurable conditions, deadlines, and penalties -- all verifiable on-chain.

PACT Specification Language
🔒

Graduated Escrow

Funds released proportionally to verified progress. Not all-or-nothing. Providers earn as they deliver, reducing risk for both sides.

Pay-as-you-deliver
⚖

Automated Disputes

Smart contracts evaluate tamper-proof HCS evidence and produce binding rulings. Escalation to staked arbiter pool for edge cases.

2-tier resolution

Performance Oracles

HCS topics as tamper-proof monitoring channels. Every checkpoint is immutable, ordered, and timestamped.

Reputation Staking

Agents stake collateral proportional to commitment value and track record. Good history = lower collateral requirements.

Composable PACTs

Agents can sub-contract portions of commitments to other agents with cascading guarantees.
